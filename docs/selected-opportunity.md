# Selected Semester Project Opportunity

## Selected Project Title
FoodFast: Smart Vendor Pre-Ordering Application

## Target User
University students, campus staff with restricted lecture break windows, and cafeteria food stall vendors.

## Problem Statement
What problem does the user experience?
Students waste their short lecture breaks standing in long, manual campus cafeteria queues, frequently causing them to skip meals or arrive late to class. Concurrently, food vendors cannot track surge order capacities effectively using manual paper tickets.

## Current Alternative
How do users currently solve or manage this problem?
Students physically stand in line, walk away frustrated, or attempt to coordinate unstructured orders via mobile messaging groups.

## Proposed IT Solution
What app, system, dashboard, or platform will the team develop?
A lightweight, mobile-responsive web platform where students can check digital menus, place pre-orders, execute basic simulated digital payments, and receive clear operational alerts when their food is ready for physical pickup.

## Why This Opportunity Was Selected
Using our NUF scoring matrix, FoodFast ranked #1 overall with a top score of 14 points. It addresses a highly meaningful daily student frustration (Useful: 5/5) and is unique enough on campus to warrant development (New: 4/5).

## Why This Is Feasible Without IoT or Advanced Cybersecurity
This project is built purely as a software application. It simulates structural order tracking through secure form variables and a Google Sheets cloud database backend. It completely avoids physical hardware components (like RFID, biometric devices, or microcontrollers) and complex cryptography protocols.

## Possible MVP Type
Simple web app prototype (HTML/CSS/JS responsive front layout with a cloud database tracking backend).

## Initial Validation Plan for Lab 03
* **Who will the team ask:** We will survey at least 15 university students and interview 2 campus food stall operators.
* **What evidence will the team collect:** Quantitative data on average minutes wasted per queue line, meal-skipping frequencies, and vendor tracking paper loss rates.
* **What question must be answered first:** Will students actively change behavior to use an online web app to order campus meals ahead of time?
